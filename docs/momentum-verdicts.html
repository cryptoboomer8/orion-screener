Momentum Verdicts

Every coin on the tracker gets tagged with one of four labels. This page explains what each means, how it’s computed, and when to act on it. Click any card to trace its path through the decision tree.

How a coin lands on a verdict

Click a verdict card below to highlight its path through the flow.

The Late filter runs first so stretched or pumpy setups never reach the positive buckets. Anything that survives gets tested for Breakout, Building, and Igniting in turn — a coin lands in the strongest label its numbers support.

The four verdicts

Side-by-side

Verdict Timeframe pattern Funding 24h range position Action
Building Cascading up, each window bigger Negative or calm Mid or breaking higher Enter if chart is clean
Breakout Strong 1h, volume-led Slightly positive OK Usually high (move is running) Join continuation
Igniting 1h leading, longer frames mixed Neutral Anywhere Watch, don’t chase
Late / Avoid Longer frames already big, 1h flat Overheated / crowded Pinned at 24h high Skip trend trades

Typical lifecycle of a momentum coin

Step 1 Igniting 1h turns positive, longer frames lagging. Mixed signals — watch, don’t commit.
Step 2 Building Cascade confirmed, OI climbing, funding calm. Entry candidates live here.
Step 3 Breakout Volume spike, 1h > 2.5%. Still trade-able if you missed Building.
Step 4 Late / Avoid Funding overheats, price pinned near 24h high. Skip further trend entries.
Ideal entry zone: the handoff from Igniting → Building is early, high-reward. Building → Breakout is the safer, higher-confirmation join.

A reminder

These labels are probabilistic, not deterministic. A coin tagged Building won’t guarantee the trade works — volume can dry up, BTC can flush, news can hit. And a handful of Late coins do extend. Use the verdicts as filters on your attention, not commands. They tell you which dozen coins of the 600+ universe deserve a chart look; your own read of the chart is the final call.
